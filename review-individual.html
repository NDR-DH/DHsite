Review for individuals and families

If something has been decided about care, support, funding, or a person's circumstances, it is not always clear whether that decision is correct or what should happen next. The situation may feel confusing, pressured, or difficult to challenge.

This route is for understanding what has happened, what framework applies, and what your options are. It is designed for situations where you need clarity before deciding what to do.

This route may be the right place to start if:

  • you have been told a decision has been made but you are not sure it is right
  • care, support, funding, or placement arrangements do not seem to make sense
  • you have concerns about capacity or best interests decisions
  • you feel something is wrong but do not yet know how to challenge it
  • you need a clear explanation of what your options are

What this usually involves

A review is a structured piece of work that looks carefully at what has happened and what should have happened. The aim is to give you a clear and grounded understanding of the situation. This is a type of work we carry out regularly for individuals and families dealing with difficult or unclear situations.

This may involve identifying the relevant legal framework, explaining how decisions are meant to be made, and looking at whether the decision you have been given fits with that framework.

What a review may involve

The purpose of the review is not to overwhelm you with technical detail, but to make the situation understandable. That may include explaining how the system works, identifying where things may have gone wrong, and setting out realistic options for what could happen next.

The outcome may be a written explanation, advice on next steps, or a clear plan for how to approach the situation.

Examples of situations we review

A care or funding decision that does not make sense

A local authority or NHS body has reduced, refused, or changed care or funding arrangements, and it is not clear what the decision is based on, whether the right assessment was carried out, or what can be done about it.

A disagreement about capacity or best interests

A decision has been made about whether someone has capacity, or about what is in their best interests, and there are concerns about whether it was handled correctly or whether the person's wishes were properly taken into account.

A situation that feels wrong but is difficult to challenge

Something has happened — involving care, placement, support, or a process — that does not seem right, but it is not clear what the rules require, whether there is a basis for challenge, or where to start.

How this work is usually structured

Clear scope

This work is focused on understanding a particular situation or decision. It is not an open-ended process.

Agreed fee

Fees are agreed in advance so you know where you stand before proceeding.

Only what is needed

We will only ask for information if it is needed for the review. There is nothing to prepare or upload at this stage.

What this can lead to

A review may show that the decision is correct, or it may show that something may not have been handled correctly. It may help you resolve the situation through discussion, or it may lead to a more formal challenge.

The purpose is to help you move forward with clarity.

If you would like a clear view of the situation before deciding what to do next, you can get in touch.
