Review for professionals and organisations

Organisations and professionals are often required to make or defend decisions in complex systems involving care, capacity, funding, and responsibility. Situations can become difficult where legal frameworks, operational pressures, and competing responsibilities intersect.

This route is for structured review of a situation or decision where clarity is needed before determining the appropriate course of action.

This route may be useful if:

  • you need an independent view on whether a decision is legally and operationally sound
  • a situation has become complex or contested and requires structured analysis
  • there is uncertainty about the correct framework or approach
  • you need to decide whether to maintain, adjust, or reconsider a position
  • a matter may escalate and requires careful handling at an early stage

What this usually involves

This work is usually focused on a defined issue, decision, or developing situation. The aim is to establish a clear and structured understanding of the position, including the relevant legal framework, the decision-making process, and the practical realities of the situation.

The purpose is not to provide general advice, but to support informed and defensible decision-making.

What a review may involve

Review work in this context often sits at the intersection of legal obligations, policy frameworks, and operational practice. The output is designed to clarify what has happened, what matters, and what options are realistically available.

This may include a written review, strategic input, or guidance on next steps.

Examples of situations we review

A funding or responsibility dispute between public bodies

Two or more public bodies are in disagreement about funding responsibility or the scope of their respective obligations, and a structured review of the legal and operational position is needed before a decision can be made or defended.

A complex care or placement decision requiring review

A decision has been made, or is being considered, about care arrangements, placement, or support for a person with complex needs, and there is a need to ensure that the correct framework has been applied and that the decision is legally sound.

A situation at risk of escalation where early clarity is needed

A situation involving a provider, family member, commissioner, or other party is becoming difficult, and early structured analysis is needed to assess the position and identify the most appropriate response before the matter develops further.

How this work is usually structured

Defined scope

Focused on a specific issue or situation. The work is bounded at the outset so the objective and output are clear.

Agreed fee

Fees are agreed in advance for the defined piece of work, so you can assess proportionality before proceeding.

Targeted input

Only the information needed for the review is requested. There is no requirement to provide material at first contact.

What this can lead to

A review may support maintaining a position, adjusting an approach, or moving toward structured resolution. It may also identify where formal processes or escalation are required.

The aim is to ensure that next steps are clear and properly grounded.

If you need a structured view of a situation before deciding how to proceed, you can get in touch.
