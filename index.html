A specialist advisory and mediation practice.

Dawson House works in mental capacity, health and social care, and disputes involving public bodies. Our work combines legal analysis, structured mediation and support for complex decision-making in situations where the institutional landscape is genuinely difficult to navigate.

We begin with understanding the situation. We identify what the relevant framework requires and what the realistic options are. We use mediation where it is appropriate and support transition to litigation where it is required.

Mental capacity

Best interests decisions, Court of Protection proceedings, capacity assessments and the legal frameworks that govern substitute decision-making.

Health and social care

NHS continuing healthcare, local authority social care commissioning, funding disputes and eligibility assessments.

Disputes involving public bodies

Challenge and representation in disputes with local authorities, NHS bodies and care providers, including pre-action and mediated resolution.

Local knowledge

The gap between rules and how they are applied

The gap between what the rules say and how they are applied is where most of the difficulty in these systems lives.

We understand the institutions involved — how decisions are made within them, where discretion sits, and where challenge is realistic.

This is not generic legal knowledge. It is the kind of local, operational understanding that only comes from sustained practice in a specific field.

It is what allows us to advise with accuracy, mediate without losing grip on what is legally possible, and litigate with precision.

It is also what allows us to explain those realities clearly — so clients can make informed decisions about what is worth pursuing, and what is not.

The practice

Dawson House is a specialist advisory and mediation practice.

We are a social enterprise, structured deliberately: to serve the people navigating these systems, and to give the specialists who practice within them the conditions in which careful, rigorous work is possible.

The quality of the work depends on how it is done — and that is not incidental to the model.
